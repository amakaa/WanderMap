Restaurant Nam Kee

12:00 - 23:00

This chinese restaurant opened in 1981 and is a perfect representation of what Dutch Chinese cuisine is all about. They have traditional Chinese things on the menu but also have dishes on the menu that is a mixture of Dutch colonial cuisines. It became locally famous because of it’s steamed oyster dish and a Dutch renowned writer named Kees van Beijnum even wrote a fictional book in 2000 with the name of the restaurant in it’s title: “The oysters of Nam Kee”.
